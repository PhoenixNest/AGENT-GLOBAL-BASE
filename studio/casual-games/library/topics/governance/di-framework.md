# Diversity & Inclusion Framework

**Owner:** CHRO Office (Dr. Evelyn Hartwell)  
**Cross-Owner:** CIO Office (automated screening audit)  
**Status:** Active  
**Effective Date:** 2026-04-12  
**Review Cadence:** Annual (or before each major hiring wave)  
**Audit Reference:** CHRO Audit Concern 3 — "No D&I Strategy" | CIO Finding — "Diversity is organic, not strategic"

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The CHRO audit identified that the current cohort of 39 FTEs/contractors is **organically diverse** but lacks a **deliberate D&I strategy**. The CIO audit independently confirmed this finding, noting that diversity in hiring outcomes is "organic, not strategic."

This is not a failure of outcome — the current team demonstrates strong demographic diversity across geography, gender, and cultural background. However, organic diversity is not reproducible or defensible at scale. As we prepare for the **3-game scaling hiring cycle** (adding 45 FTEs to reach 85 total), we must transition from organic to intentional.

This framework establishes a **lightweight, actionable D&I strategy** for the next hiring cycle. It is deliberately minimal — designed to integrate into existing recruitment workflows without creating bureaucratic overhead.

## 3. D&I Framework for Next Hiring Cycle

### 3.1 Demographic Data Collection

**What:** Voluntary, anonymized self-identification at offer acceptance stage.

**Categories Collected:**

- Gender identity
- Ethnicity/race (region-appropriate categories)
- Veteran status
- Disability status

**Guardrails:**

- Opt-out always available — no candidate is required to disclose
- Data is **aggregate only** — never used for individual hiring decisions
- Data is stored separately from personnel files, accessible only to CHRO Office and CIO Office (for audit purposes)
- Candidates may update or withdraw their data at any time

**Purpose:** Enable diversity reporting and sourcing channel effectiveness analysis. Not a quota system. Not a hiring criterion.

### 3.2 Bias Audit of Automated Screening

**What:** Quarterly review of sourcing algorithm outputs for demographic parity.

**Threshold:** No demographic group should have a **>20% lower pass rate** than the median group without a valid, documented, competency-based explanation.

**Process:**

1. CIO Office exports screening funnel data by demographic group (quarterly)
2. Calculate pass rate per stage: sourcing → screening → interview → offer
3. Flag any group with pass rate >20% below median
4. Investigate root cause: algorithmic bias, job description language, channel quality, or legitimate competency distribution
5. Remediate confirmed bias before next sourcing cycle

**Owner:** CIO Office (execution), CHRO Office (oversight)

### 3.3 Diverse Sourcing Channel Targets

**What:** Allocate at least **30% of sourcing budget** to channels that historically produce diverse candidate pools.

**Target Channels (non-exhaustive):**

- HBCU (Historically Black Colleges & Universities) partnerships
- Women-in-tech conferences and organizations (e.g., Women in Games International)
- Disability employment programs and job boards
- International game development communities (e.g., IGDA chapters in underrepresented regions)
- Refugee and immigrant professional networks
- Veterans in gaming organizations

**Tracking:** Each sourcing channel is tagged with its historical diversity yield. Channels below 10% diverse candidate yield are flagged for review. Channels above 40% are prioritized for increased allocation.

### 3.4 Inclusive Interview Panel Composition

**Requirement:** Every interview panel must include:

1. At least **one panelist from a different division** than the hiring manager (prevents divisional homophily)
2. At least **one panelist who has completed unconscious bias training** (within the last 12 months)

**Rationale:** Homogeneous panels produce homogeneous hiring decisions. Cross-divisional panels reduce in-group bias and improve candidate experience.

**Compliance:** Tracked in interview scheduling system. Panels not meeting this requirement are rejected and rescheduled.

### 3.5 Accessibility in Hiring Process

**Requirements:**

- All job postings must include an **accessibility accommodation statement** (e.g., "If you require accommodations during the interview process, please contact HR at [email]. We will work with you to ensure an equitable experience.")
- Interview platforms must support **screen readers** and **keyboard navigation**
- Technical assessments must offer **alternative formats** upon request (e.g., extended time, oral examination, pair programming instead of take-home)
- Interview scheduling must accommodate **time zone differences** for international candidates (no candidate should be required to interview outside 7am–9pm their local time)

## 5. Metrics and Reporting

### 5.1 Quarterly Diversity Dashboard

**Audience:** C-suite  
**Content:** Aggregate-only demographic data (no individual-level data)

| Metric                                   | Description                                                                          |
| ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Headcount by gender                      | Percentage breakdown across gender identities                                        |
| Headcount by ethnicity                   | Percentage breakdown across ethnic categories (region-appropriate)                   |
| New hires from diverse sourcing channels | Percentage of new hires sourced from targeted diverse channels                       |
| Interview panel compliance rate          | Percentage of panels meeting cross-divisional + bias-trained requirements            |
| Screening funnel parity                  | Pass rate by demographic group at each stage (flagged if >20% deviation from median) |

### 5.2 Annual D&I Report

**Audience:** C-suite and User (CEO)  
**Content:** Comprehensive annual report including:

- Year-over-year diversity trends
- Sourcing channel effectiveness analysis
- Bias audit findings and remediation actions
- Accessibility compliance status
- Framework refinement recommendations

### 5.3 Sourcing Channel Diversity Performance

Tracked continuously by CIO Office:

| Channel        | Diverse Candidate % | Hires from Channel | Cost per Hire | Quality Rating (90-day retention) |
| -------------- | ------------------- | ------------------ | ------------- | --------------------------------- |
| [Channel name] | [%]                 | [count]            | [$]           | [1–5]                             |

Channels rated "quality < 3" are reviewed for continued investment. Channels rated "quality ≥ 4" are prioritized for increased allocation.
