## 1. Executive Summary

### CPO Audit Condition: CR-1

**Source:** CPO Product Audit (Marcus Tran-Yoshida), Item 9 — "Community Management Capability — Ownership Gap"
**Risk ID:** CR-1
**Severity:** 🔴 P0
**Probability:** High | **Impact:** High

**Condition Statement (verbatim from CPO audit):**

> _"No dedicated Community Manager has been hired. The Live Ops Lead cannot simultaneously own strategic kill-gate decision making and day-to-day community operations during soft launch."_

**Current State:** The Casual Games studio has 38 FTEs + 1 Contract across 7 divisions. Aisha Nkemelu (Live Ops Lead, Principal-level) currently carries community strategy capability as a secondary skill — she grew Playrix's Discord from 15K to 120K members. However, she has 4 direct reports (2x Live Ops Engineers, UA Specialist, Data Analyst) and owns strategic kill-gate decision making for Stages 7, 8, and 10. Adding 20–30 hours/week of operational community management (moderation, player support tickets, social media responses, crisis communication) during soft launch would:

1. **Dilute her capacity for KPI monitoring** — D1/D7/D30 retention, ARPDAU, LTV:CAC validation at kill gates
2. **Create a kill-gate decision risk** — If Aisha is drowning in community tickets, she cannot make data-driven kill/iterate decisions at G3 (Soft Launch)
3. **Compromise community sentiment tracking** — QBR metric (< 50% positive = crisis protocol) cannot be reliably tracked without a dedicated owner

**Purpose of This Requisition:** Create a pre-approved, fully specified Community Manager position that can be executed immediately when Stage 6 (Automated Testing) approaches, ensuring the hire is onboarded and operational before Stage 7 (Soft Launch Prep) entry.

**Budget Authorization:** $80K–$110K base + 10% bonus + 0.08% equity (per CPO recommendation). Alternative agency contract option included as bridge.

## 5. Assessment Battery

### 5.1 Written Communication Test (Duration: 90 minutes)

**Task:** Draft a patch notes announcement for a hypothetical v1.2.0 update that includes:

- 3 new levels
- Economy balance changes (reduced coin cost of boosters by 15%, increased energy regeneration rate)
- Bug fix for a crash affecting 2% of players on Android 12
- Upcoming community event announcement (weekend double rewards)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
| Criterion | Weight | What We're Looking For |
| ------------------------ | ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Clarity | 30% | Can a non-technical player understand what changed and why |
| Tone | 25% | Matches casual game brand voice — friendly, transparent, enthusiastic |
| Structure | 20% | Logical organization, scannable formatting, clear section headers |
| Localization readiness | 15% | Content structured for easy translation (no idioms, clear context, key-ready) |
| Call-to-action | 10% | Drives player engagement with the update and upcoming event |

**Pass Threshold:** ≥ 70/100

### 5.2 Crisis Simulation (Duration: 60 minutes)

**Scenario:** You are the Community Manager for a casual game that just launched a soft update. The update accidentally introduced a bug that:

- Causes the game to crash on startup for ~5% of iOS players
- The bug was not caught in QA because it only affects devices with a specific iOS version
- Players are posting angry messages on Discord, Reddit, and Twitter
- Some players are demanding compensation for lost play time and in-game purchases
- A gaming news outlet has picked up the story with the headline: "Popular Casual Game Breaks After Latest Update"

**Task:** Within 60 minutes, produce:

1. An initial acknowledgment post (for Discord and Twitter) — within 30 minutes of the scenario start
2. A follow-up status update (once engineering has identified the root cause and ETA for fix)
3. A compensation proposal for affected players (with rationale)
4. A post-incident communication plan (how you'll rebuild trust)

**Evaluation Criteria:**
| Criterion | Weight | What We're Looking For |
| ------------------------ | ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Speed of response | 20% | Acknowledges the issue quickly and transparently |
| Tone under pressure | 25% | Calm, accountable, empathetic — not defensive or dismissive |
| Escalation judgment | 20% | Knows when to involve engineering, Live Ops Lead, and Studio Director |
| Compensation reasoning | 20% | Fair, proportional, doesn't set unsustainable precedent |
| Recovery plan | 15% | Concrete steps to rebuild trust, not just apologies |

**Pass Threshold:** ≥ 70/100

### 5.3 Community Analytics Review (Duration: 45 minutes)

**Task:** Review a provided community sentiment dashboard (simulated data) showing:

- Discord sentiment trends over 30 days (positive/negative/neutral)
- Top 10 discussion topics by volume
- Support ticket categories and volume trends
- Social media engagement rates

The data shows:

- Sentiment declining from 78% positive to 62% positive over 30 days
- Rising complaints about "too many ads" and "energy system too restrictive"
- Support ticket volume up 40% week-over-week, primarily about in-app purchase issues
- Twitter engagement down 25% despite increased posting frequency

**Deliverable:** A 1-page analysis with:

1. Root cause hypothesis for sentiment decline
2. Recommended immediate actions (next 48 hours)
3. Recommended strategic actions (next 30 days)
4. Metrics to track for improvement

**Evaluation Criteria:**
| Criterion | Weight | What We're Looking For |
| ------------------------ | ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Data interpretation | 30% | Correctly identifies patterns and correlations in the data |
| Prioritization | 25% | Distinguishes urgent vs. important actions |
| Actionability | 25% | Recommendations are specific, feasible, and tied to root causes |
| Metric selection | 20% | Chooses the right metrics to track improvement |

**Pass Threshold:** ≥ 70/100

### 5.4 Behavioral Interview (Duration: 45 minutes)

**Conducted by:** Aisha Nkemelu (Live Ops Lead) + CHRO representative

**Core Questions:**

1. _"Tell me about the most toxic community situation you've managed. What was the root cause, what did you do, and what would you do differently?"_
2. _"Describe a time when player feedback led to a meaningful product change. How did you surface the signal from the noise?"_
3. _"You notice a steady decline in community sentiment, but the data team says all KPIs are on track. What do you do?"_
4. _"How do you balance being the 'voice of the studio' (protecting brand) with being the 'voice of the players' (advocating for their concerns)?"_
5. _"Walk me through your process for building a community from scratch for a new game launch."_

**Evaluation Criteria:**
| Criterion | Weight | What We're Looking For |
| ------------------------ | ------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Self-awareness | 25% | Acknowledges mistakes, demonstrates learning |
| Strategic thinking | 25% | Thinks beyond reactive moderation to proactive community building |
| Cross-functional skill | 25% | Demonstrates ability to work with engineering, design, and leadership |
| Cultural fit | 25% | Aligns with studio values: creative excellence, player-first, data-informed |

**Pass Threshold:** ≥ 70/100

### Overall Assessment Gate

A candidate must pass **all four assessments** with a composite score of ≥ 70/100 to proceed to the vetting gate. Failure in any single assessment can be waived if the candidate scores ≥ 80 in the other three, subject to CHRO approval.
