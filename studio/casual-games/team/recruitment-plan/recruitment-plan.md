## Core Philosophy

**Build for creative excellence first, then operational scale.** A game studio is not a standard software engineering organization — it requires deep creative talent (artists, designers, audio engineers) working in lockstep with engineers who understand the unique constraints of real-time interactive entertainment. Every hire must demonstrate both craft mastery AND the ability to collaborate across creative/technical boundaries.

**Ship vertical slices, not feature lists.** The 11-stage pipeline is designed around iterative creative validation — Art Direction (Stage 0) informs Concept (Stage 1), which informs Prototype (Stage 2), which is validated by a Vertical Slice (Stage 3) before full production commitment. Hiring must ensure that the team capable of producing the vertical slice is fully staffed **before** Production Planning (Stage 4).

**Live ops is a first-class discipline.** Post-launch operations (Stage 10) are not an afterthought — they are the revenue engine. The Live Ops team must be hired **before** Soft Launch (Stage 8), not after. Community management, content pipeline, and economy balancing require dedicated specialists who understand the cadence of live game operations.

**Security and compliance remain non-negotiable.** The game studio inherits all pipeline security requirements from the parent company's 10-stage pipeline (SRD, MASVS, data privacy, anti-cheat). Every engineer, designer, and artist must understand baseline security principles.

### Span-of-Control Constraint

**Every manager has ≤8 direct reports.** This constraint has been **accepted by the User** and is enforced as a hard limit during all hiring phases. It is stricter than the parent company's engineering standard (≤10), reflecting game development's creative collaboration intensity and the need for frequent cross-disciplinary syncs (art reviews, design critiques, playtests).

**Enforcement mechanism:** If a hiring wave would push any manager above 8 direct reports, a sub-lead or additional manager is hired first. No exceptions.

## 5. Audio Division — 1 FTE + 1 Contract

**Purpose:** Sound design, music composition, audio implementation, voice direction

| Role                          | Count        | Level     | Responsibilities                                                                | Pipeline Stages |
| ----------------------------- | ------------ | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------- |
| **Audio Designer**            | 1 (FTE)      | Senior    | Sound effects, chiptune synthesis, FMOD/Wwise integration, audio implementation | 2, 3, 5, 6      |
| **Composer / Sound Director** | 1 (Contract) | Principal | Original music score, audio direction, audio pipeline design                    | 0, 1, 2, 5      |

**Why This Structure:**

- **Audio Designer** is the primary audio production role — handles SFX synthesis (chiptune waveforms), FMOD/Wwise adaptive audio design, and Unity integration
- **Composer / Sound Director** is hired on a **per-title contract basis** — they compose original chiptune music and define the audio direction, then hand off stems to the Audio Designer for integration
- Chiptune synthesis requires **no recording hardware, no acoustic treatment, no studio space** — all audio is generated from software (Deflemask, FamiTrack, DAW)
- **1 FTE + 1 contract** handles all audio for one mobile game; the contract composer can support 2–3 games with staggered schedules

**Adaptability:**

- For 3 concurrent games: Audio scales to 4–5 FTEs (shared composer, per-game audio designer)
- Music licensing: Composer can license external music tracks when original composition capacity is insufficient

- **5 FTEs** handle live ops for one mobile game; they can support 2 games with staggered launch schedules

**Adaptability:**

## Compensation Strategy

Game industry talent commands a **15–25% premium** over equivalent app engineering benchmarks due to specialized skill scarcity (Unity/DOTS, technical art, game design) and the emotional/creative investment required.

### Salary Ranges (USD, annual, base — excludes bonus/equity)

| Seniority    | App Benchmark | Game Premium (15–25%) | Proposed Range  |
| ------------ | ------------- | --------------------- | --------------- |
| VP / Head-of | $220K–$320K   | +20–25%               | **$265K–$400K** |
| Director     | $180K–$260K   | +20–25%               | **$215K–$325K** |
| Staff / Lead | $155K–$210K   | +18–22%               | **$185K–$255K** |
| Senior IC    | $130K–$175K   | +15–20%               | **$150K–$210K** |
| Mid IC       | $100K–$140K   | +15–18%               | **$115K–$165K** |
| Junior IC    | $75K–$100K    | +15%                  | **$85K–$115K**  |

### Role-Specific Adjustments

| Role                       | Adjustment      | Rationale                                                          |
| -------------------------- | --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Technical Artists          | +10% above band | Extreme scarcity — fewer than 2,000 qualified TAs in North America |
| Unity DOTS/ECS Specialists | +8% above band  | Niche skill; most Unity devs work in MonoBehaviour                 |
| Lead Game Designer         | Top of band     | Design quality is the #1 predictor of casual game success          |
| QA Manual Testers          | Bottom of band  | High supply; retain through career path to QA Automation           |

### Retention Levers

- **Revenue share** on shipped titles (0.5–2% per team member, scaled by contribution)
- **Shipping bonus** ($5K–$15K per title launched)
- **Flexible remote/hybrid** — 3 days in-studio for creative reviews, 2 days remote
- **Game jam time** — 2 weeks per quarter for experimental projects
- **No-crunch policy** — published and enforced; part of employer brand from Day 1

## Contingency Planning — Kill Gate Scenarios

The game studio operates under a **kill gate protocol** — if specific criteria are not met at designated checkpoints, the project is terminated and resources are reallocated. This protects the company from sunk-cost fallacy.

### Kill Gate Checkpoints

| Gate | Stage                     | Criteria                                                                                                          | Decision Authority        | Action if Failed                                            |
| ---- | ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| G1   | Stage 3 (Vertical Slice)  | Vertical slice does not meet quality bar; core gameplay loop is not fun; art style does not resonate in playtests | User + Studio Director    | Terminate project; redeploy team to other titles or release |
| G2   | Stage 5 (Full Production) | Production is >30% over budget or >50% over schedule; technical debt is unmanageable                              | User + Executive Producer | Reduce scope or terminate                                   |
| G3   | Stage 8 (Soft Launch)     | D1 retention < 25%; D7 retention < 8%; ARPDAU < $0.05; crash rate > 2%                                            | User + Live Ops Lead      | Iterate (30 days) or terminate                              |
| G4   | Stage 9 (Global Launch)   | Soft launch KPIs not improved after iteration; store rating < 3.5 stars                                           | User + Studio Director    | Do not launch globally; sunset or pivot                     |
| G5   | Stage 10 (Live Ops)       | Month 3 revenue < $50K; D30 retention < 2%; community sentiment is negative                                       | User + Executive Producer | Sunset game; redeploy team                                  |

### Team Redeployment Plan

If a project is terminated, the following redeployment strategy applies:

| Division            | Redeployment Path                                                                                           |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Engineering (15)    | Reassign to other game projects or parent company's R&D division; 80% retention target                      |
| Creative/Design (5) | Reassign to other game projects; Lead Game Designer may join new game's concept phase                       |
| Art (6)             | Reassign to other game projects; contract artists released immediately                                      |
| Audio (2)           | Reassign to other game projects; composer retains IP rights per contract                                    |
| Production (2)      | Reassign to other game projects or parent company                                                           |
| Live Ops (3)        | Reassign to other live games; Live Ops Lead may join new game's soft launch prep                            |
| Leadership (3)      | Studio Director → VP of Studios or new game; Creative Director → new game concept; Exec Producer → new game |

### Hiring Freeze Trigger

If **two consecutive kill gates** are triggered across the studio's portfolio, a **hiring freeze** is enacted. No new hires are authorized until:

1. A post-mortem is conducted for each failed project
2. Lessons learned are documented and integrated into the recruitment competency matrices
3. The User authorizes resumption of hiring

## Pipeline Optimizations for Game Studio

The parent company's 10-stage pipeline was designed for enterprise mobile applications. The game studio's 11-stage pipeline requires the following optimizations:

### 1. Stage 0 Pre-Production Gate

| Current (10-stage)     | Game Studio (11-stage)                                                                             |
| ---------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Stage 1 = Requirements | Stage 0 = Art Direction precedes requirements; visual pillars inform GDD and PRD                   |
| **Rationale**          | In game development, art style is a foundational creative decision that shapes all downstream work |

### 2. Vertical Slice as Go/No-Go Gate (Stage 3)

| Current (10-stage)     | Game Studio (11-stage)                                                                                                                                      |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Stage 3 = Architecture | Stage 3 = Vertical Slice (fully polished sample of the final game)                                                                                          |
| **Rationale**          | The vertical slice is the single most important artifact in game development — it validates fun, quality, and feasibility before full production investment |

### 3. Soft Launch as Extended Validation (Stage 8)

| Current (10-stage)    | Game Studio (11-stage)                                                                                                                |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Stage 6 = Code Review | Stage 8 = Soft Launch (30–90 days of real player data)                                                                                |
| **Rationale**         | Games require real player validation — soft launch provides D1/D7/D30 retention, ARPDAU, and engagement data that cannot be simulated |

### 4. Live Ops as Continuous Stage (Stage 10)

| Current (10-stage)           | Game Studio (11-stage)                                                                                       |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Stage 10 = Release Readiness | Stage 10 = Live Ops (continuous content updates, events, economy tuning)                                     |
| **Rationale**                | Mobile F2P games generate 80%+ of revenue post-launch — live ops is not an endpoint, it's the business model |

### 5. Cross-Functional Art Reviews

| Current (10-stage)          | Game Studio (11-stage)                                                              |
| --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Design reviews by CDO panel | Art reviews by Creative Director + Art Director + Studio Director + CDO             |
| **Rationale**               | Game art requires both creative quality assessment AND technical feasibility review |

### 6. Playtest Integration

| Current (10-stage)      | Game Studio (11-stage)                                                                                  |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| User testing at Stage 6 | Playtests at Stage 2 (prototype), Stage 3 (vertical slice), Stage 5 (production), Stage 8 (soft launch) |
| **Rationale**           | Fun cannot be validated through code review — it requires iterative playtesting with real players       |

## Recommendation

**Hire all 37 FTEs across 4 phased waves** upon candidate roster approval by all Chief Officers, Studio Director, and the User.

**Hiring Model:** Staggered 4-Phase — Phase 1: Leadership (Weeks 1–4), Phase 2: Creative + Production (Weeks 4–8), Phase 3A/B: Engineering + Art (Weeks 8–16), Phase 4: Live Ops (Weeks 16–20).

**Elite Hiring Standard:** Every candidate must meet or exceed the bar set by existing personnel. The 20-point vetting gate (Impact at Scale, Craft Depth, Leadership Signal, Standards Signal, Red Flag Scan) applies to every role — no exceptions, no compromises, no "good enough for now" hires. A vacant position is preferable to a substandard fill. **Cultural alignment is a non-negotiable minimum bar** — creative or technical excellence cannot compensate for cultural misalignment.

**Distributed Vetting:** All relevant Chief Officers and studio leadership participate in the vetting process. The CHRO cannot vet 36 candidates alone. Each officer owns their domain assessment, and unanimous approval is required for every hire.

**Rationale:**

1. **Phased hiring ensures creative and production leadership precedes IC onboarding** — every artist, designer, and engineer has clear creative direction from Day 1
2. **Stage 3 vertical slice gate** — the team capable of producing the vertical slice is fully staffed before production commitment
3. **Live ops hired before soft launch** — the revenue engine is staffed before revenue begins
4. **Competitive advantage:** Most mobile game studios under-invest in engineering and QA — this studio will ship higher-quality games faster
5. **Kill gate protection** — explicit kill criteria at Stages 3, 5, 8, 9, and 10 prevent sunk-cost fallacy
6. **3-game scaling model** — the base studio is designed to scale to 3 concurrent titles with shared infrastructure
7. **Cross-functional collaboration** — technical artist, UX writer, and economy designer bridge creative-technical boundaries

**Expected Outcome:** 1 shipped mobile game per 6–9 months; 3 concurrent games at full studio capacity; D1 retention > 40%; D7 retention > 15%; ARPDAU > $0.10
