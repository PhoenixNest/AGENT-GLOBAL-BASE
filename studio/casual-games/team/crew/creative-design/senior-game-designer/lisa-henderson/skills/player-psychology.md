## Scenarios & Trade-offs

### Scenario 1: Difficulty Curve Based on Player Psychology

- **Approach:** Design flow channel — alternating challenge and relief; use dynamic difficulty adjustment based on player performance
- **Trade-off:** Challenge vs. accessibility — some players want constant challenge, others want relaxation
- **Quality Bar:** Players report feeling "just challenged enough"; churn at difficulty spikes reduced by ≥ 20%

### Scenario 2: Retention Through Psychological Drivers

- **Approach:** Address all three SDT needs — autonomy (meaningful choices), competence (visible progress), relatedness (social features)
- **Trade-off:** Engagement vs. addiction — ethical design respects player time and wellbeing
- **Quality Bar:** D1 retention ≥ 40%, D7 ≥ 20%, D30 ≥ 10%; positive player sentiment in reviews

## Quality Standards

- All design decisions backed by player psychology principles
- Behavioral data collected and analyzed for design iteration
- Ethical design principles followed (no exploitative patterns)
- Player wellbeing considered (session length reminders, spending limits)
- Design documents include psychological rationale for key features
