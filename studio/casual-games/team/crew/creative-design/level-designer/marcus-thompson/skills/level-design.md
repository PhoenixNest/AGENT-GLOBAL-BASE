# Level Design

**Skill ID:** level-design

## Overview

Level design for casual mobile games — layout design, pacing, spatial storytelling, and player guidance through environmental design.

## Scenarios & Trade-offs

### Scenario 1: 200-Level Campaign with Progressive Difficulty

- **Approach:** Design difficulty curve with 3-beat rhythm (easy-medium-hard); introduce new mechanics every 10 levels; provide difficulty variants for struggling players
- **Trade-off:** Pacing vs. content volume — more levels = more content but may dilute quality
- **Quality Bar:** Players feel consistent progression; no level causes > 30% churn; new mechanics introduced at appropriate pace

### Scenario 2: Player Guidance Through Spatial Design

- **Approach:** Use visual hierarchy, lighting, color, and composition to guide player attention without explicit instructions
- **Trade-off:** Subtle guidance vs. clarity — too subtle = confusion, too obvious = patronizing
- **Quality Bar:** ≥ 80% of players find optimal path without hints; player feedback indicates levels feel "intuitive"

## Quality Standards

- Each level designed with clear objective, optimal path, and alternative solutions
- Difficulty progression mathematically modeled and validated through playtesting
- Level design documents include pacing maps, mechanic introduction schedule, and expected completion time
- All levels tested on target devices for performance and playability
- Telemetry designed into every level (completion time, retry count, exit points)
