## Scenarios & Trade-offs

### Scenario 1: Obstacle Variety Across 200 Levels

- **Approach:** Design 15 core obstacle types with 3–5 variations each; introduce obstacles gradually; combine obstacles for complexity
- **Trade-off:** Variety vs. mastery — too many obstacle types prevent player mastery; too few become repetitive
- **Quality Bar:** Players can explain each obstacle's function after first encounter; obstacle combinations create emergent complexity

### Scenario 2: Dynamic Difficulty Adjustment

- **Approach:** Monitor player performance in real-time; adjust obstacle placement, resource availability, or time limits based on player behavior
- **Trade-off:** Personalized experience vs. predictable design — dynamic adjustment can feel inconsistent
- **Quality Bar:** Struggling players receive subtle assistance without noticing; skilled players remain challenged; overall churn reduced by ≥ 15%

## Quality Standards

- Each encounter designed with clear failure conditions and success criteria
- Obstacle difficulty validated through playtesting (target completion rate: 60–80% on first attempt)
- Encounters designed with multiple solution paths where appropriate
- All encounter parameters documented and version-controlled
- Dynamic difficulty adjustment rules transparent and ethical
