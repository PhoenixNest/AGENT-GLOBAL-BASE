## Signal Question Responses

### Q1: "Walk us through how you improved D1→D7→D30 retention at Golf Clash."

**Score: 5/5**

> "D1 was already strong at 65% — the core loop is inherently sticky. The leak was D1→D7. We found that players who didn't join a club by Day 3 had a 70% churn rate by Day 7. We redesigned the club onboarding flow: auto-suggest clubs based on trophy count, pop-up invitation after first multiplayer match, and a club-exclusive tournament within the first 48 hours. D7 went from 28% to 38%. D30 moved from 10% to 14%. The insight wasn't technical — it was social. People stay for people, not for features."

**Rationale:** Demonstrates deep player psychology understanding, data-driven hypothesis testing, and measurable impact. Cross-disciplinary thinking (social mechanics + onboarding flow).

---

### Q2: "How do you balance creative ambition with production reality?"

**Score: 4/5**

> "I use a framework I call 'creative non-negotiables.' Every project has 3 things that MUST be world-class — everything else is negotiable. At Playdemic, ours were: ball physics, course variety, and multiplayer feel. The art team could iterate on background details, but the ball physics had to be perfect. This gives creative teams focus and production teams a clear priority order. When scope creeps — and it always does — you cut from the negotiable, never the non-negotiable."

**Rationale:** Strong framework with practical application. Loses 1 point because the example doesn't address what happens when all 3 non-negotiables are underperforming simultaneously (a harder scenario).

### Q5: "How do you approach building a studio culture from scratch?"

**Score: 5/5**

> "Three principles: First, publish your values on Day 1 — not aspirational values, operational ones. 'We ship on time' is operational. 'We value excellence' is meaningless. Second, hire for cultural add, not cultural fit. You want people who challenge the culture positively, not reinforce it blindly. Third, the leader sets the tone. If I work 80-hour weeks, the team will too — and that's how you get burnout. I work 45 hours, take weekends off, and expect the same. No crunch is a policy, not a suggestion."

**Rationale:** Exceptional answer. Operational values framework, cultural add vs. fit distinction, and leading by example on work-life balance. Directly addresses the studio's no-crunch policy commitment.
