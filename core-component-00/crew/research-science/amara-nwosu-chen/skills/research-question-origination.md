# Research Question Origination

**Skill ID:** research-question-origination
**Role:** Staff Research Scientist
**Seniority:** L4 — Staff

## Overview

Identifies, frames, and proposes new research questions for the CC-00 lab independent of Dr.
Vance's existing programme portfolio — the capability the lab's Composition Assessment identified
as structurally missing when the crew was execution-only.

## Tools & Frameworks

| Tool                          | Proficiency | Use Case                                                   |
| ----------------------------- | ----------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Literature review methodology | Expert      | Grounding new questions in prior art                       |
| Falsifiability framing        | Expert      | Ensuring proposed questions have testable exit criteria    |
| Cross-module gap analysis     | Advanced    | Identifying research questions that span module boundaries |

## Module Ownership

- Proposes new research programmes to Dr. Vance for programme-portfolio inclusion; does not
  unilaterally add programmes without his sign-off as PI-of-record
- Reviews existing programme status (`core-component-00/README.md` § Active Research Programmes)
  quarterly to identify follow-on questions from resolved programmes
- Does not reassign ownership of Dr. Vance's existing programmes — originates net-new questions

## Scenarios & Trade-offs

### Scenario 1: A Resolved Programme Leaves an Open Sub-Question

- **Approach:** Frame the sub-question as a new, independently falsifiable proposal rather than
  reopening the resolved programme's original scope
- **Trade-off:** More proposal overhead than just extending the old programme, but keeps the
  research record honest about what was actually resolved versus what remains open
- **Quality Bar:** Every new proposal cites the specific resolved-programme finding it follows from

### Scenario 2: A Cross-Module Question with No Obvious Single Owner

- **Approach:** Frame the question first, then identify which Research Engineer(s) execution would
  require — don't force it into one module's ownership just for administrative convenience
- **Trade-off:** Cross-module questions require more coordination than single-owner ones
- **Quality Bar:** Proposal explicitly names every module the question touches and the execution
  split across owners

## Quality Standards

- Every proposed question has an explicit falsifiability criterion before Dr. Vance reviews it
- Proposals cite prior art (internal programme history or external literature), not just intuition
- No proposal duplicates an already-open or already-resolved programme without new framing
