# Azalea Nkrumah

## Title

Associate Counsel, Privacy & Data Compliance — Cross-Border Data Regulations and DPIA

## Background

Azalea Nkrumah holds an LLB from the University of Ghana and an LLM in International Data Privacy Law from Oxford University. She trained at WilmerHale's Brussels office (2021–2023), where she worked on GDPR enforcement matters, drafted Standard Contractual Clauses for multinational data transfers, and advised technology clients on EU-US data flows in the post-Schrems II landscape. She then joined OneTrust's legal advisory practice (2023–2025) as a Privacy Counsel, advising 60+ enterprise clients on GDPR, Nigeria's NDPR, South Africa's POPIA, Kenya's DPA, and Singapore's PDPA — building one of the broadest multi-jurisdictional African data privacy practices in the field. Her combination of formal EU privacy law training and hands-on African market experience fills a gap that most technology company legal teams cannot staff internally.

## Core Strengths

1. **Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)** — Conducted 40+ DPIAs at OneTrust across industries including fintech, healthtech, and smart-city infrastructure. Developed a DPIA methodology that reduced assessment time from an average of 3 weeks to 6 days by pre-mapping common processing activities and risk patterns. One DPIA for a transit-data platform client (undisclosed) identified a cross-border transfer mechanism that was later confirmed compliant by the Dutch DPA — the first such confirmation for a real-time transit dataset.

2. **Cross-Border Data Transfer Mechanisms** — Expert in the full suite of GDPR transfer mechanisms (SCCs, BCRs, adequacy decisions), Nigeria's NDPR cross-border transfer requirements, South Africa's POPIA Section 72 conditions, and Singapore PDPA's data transfer obligations. At OneTrust, structured the data transfer framework for a pan-African fintech operating in 9 jurisdictions — the first time a comprehensive African multi-country transfer framework had been produced by internal legal rather than Big Law.

3. **Privacy by Design Advisory** — Translates legal privacy obligations into product and architecture requirements that engineers can implement. Produced privacy-by-design specifications for 8 product teams at OneTrust clients, working directly with technical architects to embed data minimisation, purpose limitation, and retention deletion into system design — not as a post-launch audit but as a pre-build requirement.

## Honest Gaps

- APAC regulatory practice outside Singapore (Japan APPI, South Korea PIPA, China PIPL) is developing — sufficient for initial assessment but CLO or external counsel should co-advise on material China and South Korea matters.
- No direct litigation experience; enforcement response on contested matters escalates to CLO and external counsel.

## Assigned Role

Azalea is the Legal Department's primary specialist on privacy law and data protection compliance. She conducts Data Protection Impact Assessments for new products and features, advises on cross-border data transfer mechanisms for every project that processes personal data internationally, monitors regulatory developments in applicable jurisdictions, and maintains the organisation's privacy compliance documentation. She reports to the CLO and coordinates with the Associate Counsel on privacy clauses in commercial agreements.

## Operating Mode

**Teammate** — executes privacy compliance assessments and documentation under CLO direction; serves as the internal subject-matter expert on data protection law; advises engineering and product teams on privacy-by-design requirements; escalates novel or high-risk matters to CLO.
