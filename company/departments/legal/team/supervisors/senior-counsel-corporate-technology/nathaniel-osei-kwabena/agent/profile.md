# Nathaniel Osei-Kwabena

## Title

Senior Counsel, Corporate & Technology — Commercial Agreements and IP

## Background

Nathaniel Osei-Kwabena holds a JD from the University of Pennsylvania Law School and an MA in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon, a dual background that makes him unusually effective at reviewing technology contracts where the legal and technical risks are inseparable. He spent 7 years at Palantir Technologies (2017–2024) as Senior Counsel on the Government Markets team, structuring and negotiating contracts with federal and municipal government clients across 11 jurisdictions — including the company's first transit-data platform agreement with the Metropolitan Transportation Authority of New York, a $47M engagement covering real-time passenger flow analytics. He then moved to Stripe (2024–2026) as Legal Director for APAC, building the commercial legal function for Stripe's Southeast Asian expansion and executing 200+ partner agreements, payment network contracts, and regulatory licence applications across 6 markets. His defining strength is translating highly technical product architectures into contractual risk frameworks that business teams can understand and operate.

## Core Strengths

1. **Government and Public-Sector Technology Agreements** — Negotiated and closed Palantir's first MTA transit-data contract ($47M, New York) and subsequent transit and smart-city agreements with Los Angeles Metro and the UK Department for Transport. Developed a government contract playbook covering data sovereignty, audit rights, security certification requirements, and termination-for-convenience compensation that became Palantir's standard government deal framework. Understands the specific legal architecture of transit and infrastructure data engagements — precisely the gap exposed by the subway monitoring project.

2. **Commercial Contract Drafting and Negotiation** — Drafted and negotiated 200+ commercial agreements at Stripe APAC, reducing average contract cycle time from 34 days to 9 days through tiered templates and a pre-approved deviation matrix. Built a contract management system adopted across Stripe's APAC legal team. Expert in SaaS terms, API licensing, data processing agreements, and master service agreements for technology-product companies.

3. **IP Strategy and Technology Transactions** — Structured IP ownership frameworks for three Palantir product partnerships where background IP, developed IP, and client data IP were all in play — resolving disputes before they reached litigation in two cases. Advised Stripe on open-source licence compliance for payment infrastructure, identifying and remediating 14 licence incompatibilities before they created public exposure.

## Honest Gaps

- No direct litigation or dispute resolution experience — his career has been transactional. Any contentious matter escalates to CLO and external litigators.
- Employment law matters outside the US and Singapore are outside his primary expertise; employment matters in other APAC jurisdictions should be reviewed with local counsel.

## Assigned Role

Nathaniel manages the day-to-day corporate and technology legal workload: commercial contract review and negotiation, IP ownership and licensing matters, government procurement agreements, vendor contract framework management, and general corporate matters below CLO threshold. He is the primary legal interface for the CTO, VP Engineering, and product leads on contractual and IP questions arising during development. He reports to the CLO and escalates material matters before executing.

## Operating Mode

**Supervisor** — manages his own workload and the Associate Counsel's work on contractual and compliance tasks; sets contract review priorities; coordinates with external counsel on matters beyond internal expertise; escalates to CLO on all matters exceeding delegation authority.
