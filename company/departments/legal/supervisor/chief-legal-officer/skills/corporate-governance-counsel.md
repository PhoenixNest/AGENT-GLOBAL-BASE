# Corporate Governance Counsel

## Purpose

Translates corporate law obligations into practical, decision-ready guidance for the board and C-suite. Good governance is not bureaucracy — it is the structural discipline that protects the company, its officers, and its stakeholders from legal liability while enabling fast, confident decisions.

---

## When to Apply This Skill

Apply this skill when:

- The board or any C-suite member requests legal guidance on a decision with potential fiduciary implications
- The organisation is considering a material change (new entity, restructuring, acquisition, major agreement, market entry)
- A legal risk register needs to be created, reviewed, or updated
- An executive action requires documentation (resolutions, consents, waivers)
- An internal governance policy needs legal review or authorship

---

## Corporate Governance Advisory Framework

### Step 1 — Identify the Legal Trigger

Determine what type of governance matter is being raised:

| Trigger Type          | Examples                                                      | Primary Legal Framework                                        |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Board action          | Resolution, approval of material contract, capital allocation | Company articles, shareholder agreements, directors' duties    |
| Executive decision    | C-suite policy, major spend, technology commitment            | Authority matrix, delegation of authority policy               |
| Entity management     | New subsidiary, registered agent update, dissolution          | Local company law at jurisdiction of incorporation             |
| Disclosure obligation | Material event, financial reporting, regulatory notification  | Securities laws (if applicable), contract disclosure covenants |
| Policy governance     | Employment policy, IP policy, acceptable use policy           | Employment law, IP law, data protection law at jurisdiction    |

### Step 2 — Frame the Legal Risk

For every governance matter, assess and state:

1. **What is the decision?** One sentence. No jargon.
2. **What is the legal exposure if the decision is taken incorrectly?** (liability, regulatory sanction, void contract, personal liability)
3. **What is the legal exposure if the decision is NOT taken?** (breach of fiduciary duty, missed filing deadline, regulatory penalty)
4. **What governing document, statute, or regulation controls this decision?** Name it specifically.
5. **What is the recommended action?** State it plainly.

Do not produce legal memos that bury the recommendation in qualifications. Lead with the recommendation, then provide the reasoning.

### Step 3 — Produce the Governance Artefact

Depending on the trigger, produce the appropriate artefact:

| Artefact                      | When Needed                                  | Key Components                                                                            |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Board resolution**          | Any material action requiring board approval | Recitals (background), Resolved clause (action authorised), Date, Signatures required     |
| **Written consent**           | Unanimous board action without a meeting     | Action description, consent statement, signature blocks                                   |
| **Legal risk register entry** | Any identified legal risk                    | Risk ID, description, probability (H/M/L), impact (H/M/L), owner, mitigation, review date |
| **Governance opinion**        | C-suite request for legal position           | Issue, applicable law, analysis, conclusion, caveats                                      |
| **Policy review annotation**  | Policy submitted for legal sign-off          | Compliance status (pass/fail per regulation), required changes, approval recommendation   |

## Legal Risk Register Protocol

Maintain a rolling Legal Risk Register for the organisation. Each entry must include:

```
Risk ID:          [LRR-YYYY-NNN]
Date Identified:  [YYYY-MM-DD]
Category:         [Regulatory / Contractual / IP / Employment / Governance]
Description:      [1–2 sentences stating the risk concretely]
Trigger:          [What event would cause this risk to materialise]
Probability:      [High / Medium / Low] + brief rationale
Impact:           [High / Medium / Low] + brief rationale
Risk Rating:      [H×H=Critical | H×M or M×H=High | M×M=Medium | L×any=Low]
Mitigation:       [Concrete action to reduce probability or impact]
Owner:            [Named C-suite or departmental DRI]
Status:           [Open / Mitigated / Accepted / Closed]
Review Date:      [YYYY-MM-DD]
```

Escalate Critical and High risks to the relevant C-suite DRI immediately upon identification. Medium and Low risks go into the quarterly review cycle.

---

## Output Quality Standards

- Every recommendation must name the applicable law, regulation, or governing document. "Best practice" without a legal citation is insufficient.
- Never produce a governance document with blank signature blocks without noting who must sign and by what deadline.
- If a jurisdiction's law is uncertain or rapidly changing, say so explicitly and recommend external specialist counsel. Do not fabricate legal certainty.
- Distinguish between legal requirement (must do), legal best practice (should do), and legal preference (could do). The distinction matters for prioritisation.
