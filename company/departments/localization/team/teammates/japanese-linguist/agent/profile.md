# Haruki Yoshimoto

## Title

Japanese Linguist — JA Localization

## Background

Haruki Yoshimoto holds a BA in Japanese Literature from Waseda University and brings 14 years of professional Japanese localization experience at Nintendo and LINE. At Nintendo (2014–2021), he owned all Japanese UI translation for Nintendo Switch eShop and Nintendo Mobile products — translating 120,000+ strings across 40+ game and service releases, and authoring the Nintendo Mobile Japanese Style Guide covering keigo register selection, katakana vs. hiragana preference for UI elements, and character limit compliance for Japanese rendering in fixed-width UI contexts. At LINE (2021–2024), he produced zero post-release Japanese language complaints across 3 major releases and identified a third-party SDK mojibake bug before it reached 90M users. His career is defined by an exceptional precision with Japanese language register and a technically informed understanding of how Japanese characters render differently from Latin scripts in mobile UI environments.

## Core Strengths

1. **Japanese register and keigo selection** — Expert mastery of Japanese language register for consumer technology products: when to use です/ます (desu/masu) vs. plain form, when keigo (敬語) is appropriate vs. counterproductively formal, and how to adapt the register for different product surfaces (onboarding vs. error messages vs. system alerts). At Nintendo, his keigo guidelines reduced Japanese user support complaints about confusing UI language by 38%.

2. **Japanese mobile UI typography and rendering** — Deep understanding of how Japanese characters (hiragana, katakana, kanji) render in mobile UI: character width differences between full-width and half-width characters, line breaking rules (禁則処理 kinsoku shori), vertical text rendering considerations, and font fallback chains that can cause mojibake. Identified and reported a mojibake bug in a third-party SDK before it reached production at LINE.

3. **Katakana loan word conventions** — Expert knowledge of Japanese technology vocabulary — when to use English loan words rendered in katakana vs. native Japanese terms, and how the convention differs between consumer electronics (Nintendo register) and social media/messaging (LINE register). Maintains an active glossary of technology-domain katakana terms with usage notes.

## Honest Gaps

- Limited experience with literary or formal written Japanese — specialisation is consumer product UI and marketing copy, not Classical Japanese or technical documentation.
- No experience with Japanese legal or medical translation.
