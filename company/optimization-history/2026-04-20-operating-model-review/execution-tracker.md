## 1. Purpose

This tracker is the **operational counterpart** to the optimization plan. The plan defines _what_ and _who_; this tracker captures _when_ and _how it's progressing_, day by day. It exists because Plan §10 sets a "Project-level dashboard freshness — Updated daily" success target that the plan itself cannot satisfy (the plan is audit-frozen except for status columns).

**Hierarchy of truth:**

| Question                                                                    | Source of truth                                          |
| --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| What are the approved findings, owners, due dates, and acceptance criteria? | Plan §§4–6, §7                                           |
| What is the current lifecycle state (`⬜ → 🟡 → 🔵 → 🟢 → ✅`) of any step? | Plan §7 (canonical) — this tracker §3 mirrors within 24h |
| What changed today; who is blocked; what is at risk?                        | This tracker §6 (Daily Log) — append only                |
| What are the current dependency-driven sequencing decisions?                | This tracker §4                                          |
| Why was the plan modified (v1.4 → v1.5 → …)?                                | Plan §13 (Document Version History)                      |

If §3 and Plan §7 disagree, **Plan §7 wins**; this tracker is fixed within 24h to match.

## 2. Sequencing Model — Why Steps Run in This Order

The plan groups steps into 30/60/90-day buckets but does not encode intra-bucket dependencies. The tracker resolves these explicitly.

| Decision                                                                                              | Rationale                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Step 5 (pipeline base + deltas refactor) starts first** in the Days 0–30 bucket                     | Steps 1–4 all edit the four product pipeline files. If Step 5 runs after them, every edit is paid 4× then re-paid during Step 5. Step 5 first eliminates that rework. Aligns with FIND-P0-01 + FIND-P2-07 root cause: "structure first, content second." |
| **Step 1 (recruitment tier reconciliation) runs in parallel with Step 5**                             | Step 1 touches `pipeline/recruitment/pipeline.md` only — disjoint from the four product pipelines. | **Steps 2, 3, 4 wait for Step 5 to merge** before they start                                          | These are content edits to pipeline files. Doing them post-Step-5 means writing them once, against the canonical base.                                                                                                                                   |
| **Step 9 (leveling rubric) is pulled left to overlap with Step 1**                                    | Step 1 introduces tier floors (L1=12/L2=15/L3=17/L4=18/L5=19); without the rubric defining what an L1/L2/L3 actually _is_, the floors are nominal.
