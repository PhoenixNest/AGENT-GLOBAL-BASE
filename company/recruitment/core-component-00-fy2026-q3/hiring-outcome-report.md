### Finding: Leadership Signal is under-evidenced across the Phase 3 cohort

I went back to my own gate (`vet-candidate.md`) and re-read what a 4/5 Leadership Signal is
supposed to mean: _"Built or transformed a team; people they mentored are now senior elsewhere."_
Signal question: _"Name someone they grew. Where is that person now?"_

Every one of the 7 Phase 3 candidates scored 4/5 on Leadership Signal. Not one of the seven
write-ups names a person that candidate grew. What's documented instead is craft and standards
evidence — red-teaming interviews, proposing benchmark extensions, catching a bypass attempt —
real signals, but they belong under Craft Depth or Standards Signal, not Leadership. Dr. Wieczorek's
file is the clearest example: his Leadership Signal justification is "personally red-teamed every
other Phase 3 candidate," which is a craft/standards contribution, not a leadership one under my
own rubric.

**This does not change any hiring decision.** Pass criteria requires ≥4 on at least 3 of the 4
numeric dimensions plus a Red Flag pass — every one of these 11 candidates clears that on Impact,
Craft, and Standards alone, with Leadership scored generously but not load-bearing for the pass.
I am not reopening any offer. What I am flagging is a **documentation-rigor gap**: Leadership
Signal appears to have been scored by pattern-matching against seniority level rather than
interrogated against my actual signal question. That's a process finding for future cycles, not a
candidate-quality finding for this one — the underlying hires are sound; the paperwork supporting
one specific dimension is thin.

**Corrective action for the next cycle:** Any future CC-00 (or company-wide) recruitment plan I
sign off on must show the actual signal-question evidence for Leadership Signal in the candidate
write-up, not just the number. I'll be checking for it specifically.

## Addendum (2026-07-03) — Resolution Executed

The CEO reviewed this finding and directed both Dr. Vance and me to state our views and resolution
plan before execution. We agreed on two actions and both are now complete:

1. **Retroactive correction.** All 7 Phase 3 candidate files were re-reviewed against the actual
   documented evidence. One candidate's score moved to 3/5 (partial genuine evidence); six moved
   to 2/5 ("not established" — no leadership evidence exists in their records at all). Every
   correction is a visible amendment with struck-through original scores, not a silent edit.

2. **Permanent gate change.** `vet-candidate.md` now requires cited signal-question evidence for
   every numeric dimension before a score is accepted. Stage 7 Secondary Officer Review — my
   office's own gate, which missed this the first time — now checks for that evidence explicitly.

**What the correction revealed that I did not anticipate when I filed the original report:** six
of the seven corrected totals fall below the sum-based tier floor. I did not expect the scope to
be that wide when I called this "one dimension is thin." I'm recording that plainly rather than
letting my earlier, narrower framing stand as the final word.

**My ruling, exercising my joint authority over the tier floor table:** none of the 7 hires are
reopened. The Stage 5 dimension-count pass gate — the pipeline's actual pass/fail logic — was
cleared independently on Impact, Craft, and Standards for every candidate, with Leadership never
counted toward any of their original passes. The sum-floor table is a Stage 5 screening heuristic;
these candidates are past Stage 9 approval with accepted offers, and re-applying a screening
heuristic retroactively, with no change in underlying competence, would be worse management than
the paperwork gap it would be fixing. Full reasoning: `phase-summaries/phase-3-summary.md` § Amendment.

## Addendum 2 (2026-07-03) — Final Audit Follow-Up: IC-Tier Leadership Signal Floor

A subsequent final audit identified a clause I had not separately addressed: the Elite Talent Bar
table in `company/pipeline/recruitment/pipeline.md` sets a per-dimension Leadership Signal pass
threshold — ≥4/5 for supervisor roles, ≥3/5 for IC roles — marked "Auto-reject is final. No human
override." All 7 Phase 3 hires were IC-tier at time of hire. Corrected Leadership Signal scores:
six of the seven now sit at 2/5, below the 3/5 IC floor; only Wieczorek clears it at 3/5.

I addressed the sum-floor-vs-dimension-count tension in Addendum 1 above but did not, at the time,
check this separate and more absolute clause. I am closing that gap now rather than leaving it
implied.

**This does not change the ruling.** The reasoning is the same reasoning already on the record:
this is a retroactive documentation correction, not a new finding about the candidates'
competence; the candidates already passed the pipeline's live evaluation at time of hire; Stage 9
approval and offer acceptance are final; and re-applying any auto-reject threshold — sum-based or
per-dimension — after the fact, with no underlying change in what these seven people can actually
do, would substitute a paperwork technicality for the judgment the pipeline exists to produce.
"No human override" governs the automated Stage 5 gate as it runs against live candidates in real
time; it was never designed to apply retroactively to a documentation correction made after
Stage 9 closed. I am stating that limitation explicitly, on the record, rather than leaving it as
an unstated assumption behind my prior ruling.
