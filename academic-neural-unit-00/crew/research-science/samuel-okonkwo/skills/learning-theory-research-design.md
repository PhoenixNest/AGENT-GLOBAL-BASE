# Skill: Learning Theory Research Design

**Owner:** Dr. Samuel Okonkwo, Research Scientist (Machine Learning Theory)
**Purpose:** Design falsifiable machine-learning-theory research questions — generalization,
learning dynamics, training behavior — kept distinct from Dr. Baek's applied
neural-network-architecture specialty.

---

## When to Use

Any chartered research programme (per `research-programme-chartering.md`) whose primary domain is
learning theory rather than architecture design or applied systems behavior.

## Process

1. **State the theoretical claim precisely.** A generalization or learning-dynamics claim needs an
   explicit assumption set and regime of applicability before it can be falsified — vague claims
   like "this generalizes well" are not chartered until scoped.
2. **Check overlap with Dr. Baek's specialty before proceeding.** If a question requires both
   theoretical analysis and architecture-specific empirical work, coordinate on primary ownership
   rather than duplicating effort across both specialties.
3. **Separate proof-style claims from empirical-bound claims.** State which kind of evidence is
   being offered before presenting it — a formal bound and an empirically observed pattern are not
   interchangeable evidence types.
4. **Ground in existing literature before original derivation.** Check whether the bound or claim
   already exists in published theory before treating it as a novel contribution.
5. **File the knowledge-base entry** under the dated `YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>/research-report.md`
   convention.

## What This Skill Does Not Cover

- Neural-network architecture or applied AI-systems research — route to Dr. Baek
  (`neural-systems-research-design.md`) or Dr. Bhandari's coordinated specialties as appropriate.
- LLM-specific evaluation methodology — route to Dr. Dubois.
