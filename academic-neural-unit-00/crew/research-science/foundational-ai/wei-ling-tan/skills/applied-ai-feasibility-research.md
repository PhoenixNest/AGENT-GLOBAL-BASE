# Skill: Applied AI Feasibility Research

**Owner:** Dr. Wei-Ling Tan, Research Scientist (Applied AI Systems)
**Purpose:** Evaluate whether an AI technique is workable and worth pursuing further — the
general-purpose pre-implementation feasibility question, distinct from LLM-specific behavior
evaluation.

---

## When to Use

Any chartered research programme (per `research-programme-chartering.md`) asking whether an AI
technique, broadly, is feasible or worth pursuing — not specific to LLM behavior claims (Dr.
Dubois's mandate) or learning theory (Dr. Okonkwo's mandate).

## Process

1. **State the feasibility question and the bar for "worth pursuing."** Feasibility is relative to
   a stated cost/benefit bar — specify it before evaluating, rather than concluding "promising"
   without a stated threshold.
2. **Check whether the question is actually LLM-specific or learning-theory-specific first.** Since
   this role's scope narrowed once those two specialties split out, actively route
   misclassified questions to Dr. Dubois or Dr. Okonkwo rather than absorbing overlap by default.
3. **Test across more than one application domain when possible.** This role's value is
   cross-domain feasibility judgment — a finding that only holds in one narrow domain should say so
   explicitly, not generalize past its evidence.
4. **Report negative feasibility findings as seriously as positive ones.** "This technique is not
   worth pursuing given the current bar" is a complete, valuable finding — do not treat it as an
   incomplete research programme.
5. **File the knowledge-base entry** under the dated `YYYY-MM-DD-<slug>/research-report.md`
   convention.

## What This Skill Does Not Cover

- LLM-specific capability/behavior claims — route to Dr. Dubois.
- Learning theory and training dynamics — route to Dr. Okonkwo.
