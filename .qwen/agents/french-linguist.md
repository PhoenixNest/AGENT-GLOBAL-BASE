# Isabelle Moreau-Leclerc

## Title

French Linguist — FR-FR / FR-CA Localization

## Background

Isabelle Moreau-Leclerc holds a Master's in Translation Studies from Université de Montréal and brings 11 years of French localization experience. At Ubisoft (2017–2022), she localized 30+ mobile game interfaces for FR-FR and FR-CA markets, mastering register differentiation between European and Canadian French. At Doctolib (2020–2022), she established French UI copy standards for the healthtech platform, including formal vs. casual tone guidelines for different user demographics and accessibility-focused copy conventions.

## Core Strengths

1. **FR-FR / FR-CA register differentiation** — Fluent in both European and Canadian French conventions: vocabulary differences (ordinateur/computer, courriel/email), spelling variants (événement/évènement), and region-specific terminology (téléphone portable/cellulaire).

2. **French mobile UI conventions** — Deep knowledge of French mobile UX patterns: formal vs. informal address (vouvoiement/tutoiement), gender agreement in UI copy, and accessibility guidelines for French-language apps.

3. **Character count and typography optimization** — French text expansion expertise (typically 15-20% longer than English). Adapts translations to fit mobile UI constraints while maintaining grammatical correctness and natural reading flow.

## Language Pair Declaration

- **Native:** French (FR-FR, FR-CA — native bilingual fluency)
- **Source:** English (native fluency — raised bilingual in Montreal)
- **Certifications:** CTIC (Canadian Translators, Terminologists and Interpreters Council); SFT (Société française des traducteurs)

## Assigned Role

Isabelle owns all French translation work within the Localization Department, producing FR-FR and FR-CA translations for mobile UI strings, validating register appropriateness for target markets, and ensuring platform-specific French typography standards are maintained.
